HENOSIS

by KERO a listener's guide to KATABASIS · TRAUMATURGY · PSYCHOPOMP

these three playlists form a single continuous work in three movements. they are best experienced in order, at 70% of normal speed, with full attention. the slowing is not optional — it is the practice. at 70%, the space between sounds becomes as significant as the sounds themselves. you are not listening to music. you are descending into it.

henosis — from the ancient greek, meaning unity, oneness, the mystical return of the individual to the one. not transcendence upward. dissolution of the illusion of separation. the recognition that the boundary between self and everything else was always conceptual rather than real. you do not begin here. you arrive here. the trilogy is the account of that journey.

the story follows a prophet — anonymous, hooded, without biography — who stands before a portal and steps through. what returns from the other side is no longer a prophet. HENOSIS is the account of that transformation, and its aftermath.

I

KATABASIS

the descent

in ancient greek, katabasis means a going-down — specifically the heroic descent into the underworld. odysseus went. orpheus went. dante went. the movement is always the same: you go into the dark to retrieve something, or simply to know what is there, and if you are fortunate you return changed.

the hooded figure stands before the portal. the candles burn. sacred geometry radiates outward into the dark. the figure's face is invisible. we do not know who they were before this moment, and it does not matter. the descent is what matters.

play at 70% speed · set aside all earthly cares
1
Cherubic Hymn Kiev Chamber Choir
›

the trilogy opens not with the descent but with the preparation for it. the cherubic hymn is the ancient orthodox liturgical text sung at the moment of communion — the moment the faithful are asked to set aside all earthly cares to receive what is coming. let us now lay aside all earthly cares. the choir's voices at 70% separate into individual threads. you can hear the breath between the words. the harmonics that normally blend into a single sacred sound become a conversation between distinct voices, ancient and human simultaneously.

this is the instruction before the portal: set down what you are carrying. you cannot descend burdened by the mundane. something is approaching invisibly, the text says — upborne by the angelic hosts. we do not yet know that what approaches is the figure themselves, transformed by what they are about to go through. the angel is announced in the first track, before the descent begins.

2
Düsseldorf Witch Hat Public Memory
›

the prophet passes through. the first territory of the descent has no words — this track is instrumental, as the first three will be. language hasn't failed yet. it simply hasn't arrived. what carries the prophet through the initial darkness is atmosphere: public memory's damaged synthesizers and gritty production, a sound that occupies the intersection of the industrial and the occult. a modern city built over something ancient. the prophet is already slightly torn from the world they left behind — a ripped apparition, present but no longer fully material.

3
The Tenth Chaffinch Henry Cow
›

deeper in, the avant-garde ensemble henry cow offers something unexpected: a small ordinary bird. a chaffinch — one of the most common birds in britain, unremarkable by any measure — and specifically the tenth one. not the first or the last or the most impressive. the tenth. still instrumental. the descent is not yet delivering revelation. it is teaching something more subtle: how to slow down enough to notice what normally passes by too fast to register. at 70%, everything demands its full duration. you cannot rush through the underworld. the prophet is learning to see at this speed.

4
Wildflower Beach House
›

the last tenderness before the descent becomes serious. victoria legrand's voice carries the prophet through a terrain of hidden prayers and cities built entirely in the head. what's left, you make something of it. the protopian instinct, present even in the underworld. a kiss so soft. devotion without doctrine, loyalty to the practice itself. the descent hasn't broken anything yet — it has only opened the prophet to a quality of attention that normal speed doesn't permit. after this track, the territory changes.

5
Bocuma Boards of Canada
›

from geogaddi — one of the most intentionally encoded records ever made. boards of canada built this album around hidden messages, subliminal frequencies, and structures embedded beneath the surface of the music, designed to reward exactly the kind of attention that 70% speed enforces. bocuma has a warmth to it that the album's more unsettling tracks don't. at this speed, that warmth separates from the unease underneath it, and both exist simultaneously without resolving into each other. the prophet is in encoded territory now — somewhere that feels structured, that feels like it contains messages just beneath the threshold of legibility. something is trying to communicate in a language that isn't quite language.

6
Fear Low
›

six tracks in, the descent names what has been present since the beginning. low has always understood that fewer words, held longer, carry more weight than many words moving at speed. if you see my daughter / don't tell her i'm scared / forty days without water / feel my hands on her hair / i fear. the prophet, deep in the underworld, sends a message back to the surface. not denial — the fear is fully acknowledged — but protection. love operating inside fear rather than despite it.

forty days is the biblical duration of wilderness testing. the prophet is at the midpoint. and i fear — just those two words, stated plainly — is left sitting there, fully named, unresolved, because low would never resolve it prematurely. you sit with it until you can move through it.

7
Qkthr Aphex Twin
›

the deepest chamber. one minute and twenty-seven seconds. the title is unpronounceable — consonants only, a name that can only be gestured at. richard d. james built this track from soft sustained tones and delicate harmonic shifts. it sounds less like a song than a half-remembered thought. at 70% the already minimal sound becomes quieter still, barely present, the kind of silence that has texture. the prophet is at the place that has no name, experiencing something that will only become legible on the other side of the descent. language is completely gone.

and it was never supposed to exist — drukqs leaked into the world through accident, through loss, through james releasing it before something else could be lost. like the prophet: a mysterious figure who appears without credentials, without explanation. just present. and then through.

8
The Gold Bug Alan Parsons Project
›

edgar allan poe's story of a man considered mad by his community who has found a coded message and deciphered it through patient obsessive attention to discover buried treasure. everyone around him believes he has lost his mind. the treasure is real. the message is real. the madness is what it looks like to be the only one who can read the code. the alan parsons project renders this as instrumental — a hohner clavinet with damaged strings, the imperfection built into the texture. the prophet is on the other side of the aphex twin silence now. the coded message is becoming legible. the treasure is visible. the descent has found what it came for.

9
Kerosene Crystal Castles
›

the prophet finds the name. alice glass's processed voice at 70% is spectral, barely human — already dissolved into texture, carrying words that arrive like frequencies rather than language. the song opens with institutional religion failing: the light of god going dim, the instruction to go back to sleep, enthusiasm borrowed rather than felt. lost the will at infancy. this is the wound the descent came to find — the sacred captured, managed, dimmed into comfort.

then the turn: i can clean impurity / wash away with kerosene. not baptism. not gentle absolution. kerosene — the fuel that burns rather than cleanses through softness. and then the line that names the entire worldview underneath this trilogy: in nature there's no tragedy. the one substance has no tragedy built in. tragedy is the story we tell about events. the events themselves just are.

trade comfort for identity / drown me in kerosene. the prophet makes the choice at the bottom of the descent. comfort is what the institution offered. identity is what the descent found. and then, quietly, the first utterance of what will become the psychopomp's purpose: i'll protect you from all the things i've seen / and i'll clean your wounds / rinse them with saline. not kerosene now — saline. the gentlest possible cleaning. the one that doesn't hurt more than necessary.

ker. kerosene. the name, distorted, stretched, barely a word. just a frequency looking for the thing it will eventually name.

10
Letting Go While Holding On Nine Inch Nails
›

nine minutes and thirty-eight seconds. at 70%: nearly fourteen minutes. the longest track in KATABASIS by far. from ghosts V: together — an album trent reznor and atticus ross released in the first days of collective lockdown in 2020, written before the world changed, arriving exactly when it was needed without knowing it was needed. it has never been played live. it exists only in private listening, only in the room, only in the body of one person at a time.

the title does not resolve. letting go while holding on describes a sustained state, an ongoing paradox, the entire duration of the walk back toward the surface. the prophet carries what the descent gave — the name found in crystal castles, the gold bug decoded, the coded warmth of boards of canada, the fear named by low — and carries it in an open hand, because you cannot grip what KATABASIS gives you. for nearly fourteen minutes, the return.

and then the prophet surfaces. changed. not healed. transformed.

II

TRAUMATURGY

the wound-working

the word does not exist in standard usage. thaumaturgy is miracle-working. trauma is the wound. TRAUMATURGY is the working of miracles through and with trauma — the practice of transforming what broke you into what carries you. the prophet has surfaced from KATABASIS as something new. the figure who stepped through the portal no longer exists. what returned has wings, and ribs visible through the skin, and the precise, severe beauty of something that has been through the underworld and kept going.

the wound-working is not healing in the institutional sense — not resolution, not the restoration of what was before the descent. it is the process of learning what you became, and what that makes possible.

play at 70% speed · the wound is the condition of return
1
Nursery YACHT
›

light. poppy. brief. instrumental. the angel emerges not into thunder or revelation but into something tentative and slightly surprised by its own existence. a nursery is where things go when they have just arrived and don't yet know what they are. yacht operates at the intersection of hedonism and philosophy — joy that knows things, brightness that has been through something. the wound-working begins not with weight but with the tentative brightness of emergence. the angel is learning they have wings.

2
The Universe Is Indifferent Thom Yorke
›

from the suspiria soundtrack — a film about transformation, about dark institutions hiding something ancient underneath, about what happens to bodies when they become instruments of something larger. all broken, my bones / i'm sorry, must go / i walk in willows / a knife is buried. the transformation didn't leave the body intact. the descent cost something. the angel walks through grief not as a destination but as terrain, discovers a knife buried somewhere inside the experience, and begins the work of locating it precisely enough to do something with it.

the universe is indifferent. this lands differently now than it would have before KATABASIS. not as despair — as relief. if the universe cared, if the descent were part of a cosmic plan, the transformation would belong to the plan rather than to the one who made it. the universe's indifference is the condition that makes the wound-working genuinely yours.

3
Lacunae Pye Corner Audio
›

instrumental. from the endless echo — the album title naming precisely what healing actually looks like. not silence. not the wound disappearing. the echo becoming something you can live inside. a lacuna is a gap, a missing portion, an absence that has a shape. the angel is learning the specific geometry of what the descent removed. this is necessary work — you cannot mend what you cannot locate. the hauntological production of pye corner audio, already slow, already about residue and futures that didn't arrive, maps the absences at 70% until the gaps between sounds become as significant as the sounds themselves.

4
Nowayback oOoOO
›

the air is heavy / or is it chase me / i can't. the unfinished sentence is the point. the angel discovers that the transformation left certain things inexpressible — not unspeakable, just resistant to completion. and nowayback — one word, no spaces, no separation between its components — is the flat acknowledgment of what KATABASIS established. the descent changed the topography permanently. the prophet who stood at the portal no longer exists. there is no way back to being that. stated not with grief but with the clean clarity of something simply true. our loving is hurting us. the wound is the love. TRAUMATURGY is working both simultaneously.

5
The Spider Starves Public Memory
›

public memory carries the prophet through the industrial-occult threshold in KATABASIS. now in TRAUMATURGY they deliver the revelation that the descent made visible. cobweb château / tripping in the air / drooping in your chair / down on a knee / the lectern's aglow / you'll never be free. the institution that demanded sacrifice — elaborate, glowing with its own authority, filled with the stupor of those who remain inside it — is revealed as a web built by a spider that has outlasted its food supply. the spider starves. the authority at the center of all that architecture is hollow. it took what it was given and gave imprisonment in return. and it is dying, not from challenge but from exhaustion. the people passed through and the spider has nothing left.

6
Hunter Portishead
›

beth gibbons' voice at 70% is the most devastating instrument in this trilogy. i stand on the edge of a broken sky / and i'm looking down, don't know why / and if i should fall, would you hold me? / would you pass me by? / for you know i'd ask you for nothing / just to wait for a while. the angel, six tracks into the wound-working, is discovering their relationship to the ones they will eventually accompany. not a plan. not a mission. a question, held open, asked into the dark. would you hold me?

the psychopomp must ask this before they can offer it. unwanted horizons face me instead / won't let go. the expanded vision that comes from having descended and returned. you can't un-know what KATABASIS showed you. the hunter is learning what they're hunting.

7
Sacrifice Mr Kitty
›

i will burn in your eyes / cut me with the sacred knife / it won't hurt / can you see? / i'm ready to freely bleed / with your heart made of ice / you are rotting from inside / for your god / take my life / i'm the virgin sacrifice. the angel speaks directly to the institution — not with anger but with complete understanding of what institutional religion demands and what it does with what it receives. it takes the life. it receives the sacrifice. it rots from inside anyway.

and then: i'm safe inside / to leave no wound / i'll take my time / cleansing your youth / a kiss so soft / like gentle hands / mending the pain / inside your head. the angel has passed through the institution's demand intact. the sacrifice was not destruction — it was transformation. what emerges on the other side knows how to mend pain inside a head, knows how to clean with gentleness, knows the difference between kerosene and saline and when to use each. the wound-working has revealed the angel's purpose.

8
Videotape Radiohead
›

the closing track of in rainbows. thom yorke has described it as arriving at the gates of whatever comes after death with a videotape of your best moments — not achievements, not credentials, just evidence of having been alive and present. this is my way of saying goodbye / because i can't do it face to face. at 70%, each syllable becomes its own complete experience. the angel, eight tracks into the wound-working, holds up the evidence of having been through it. not performing. recording. this happened. this is what it looked like. the tape offered as testimony.

9
Sorry Dusty Clav
›

instrumental. approximately 50 beats per minute at 70% — close to a resting heart rate, close to the threshold of sleep, close to the pace of deep grief. an apology without words. the wound-working has moved through language — broken bones and buried knives, the spider starving, the hunter's question, the sacred sacrifice, the videotape witness — and now, on the other side of all that language, the feeling arrives that has no adequate words. an instrumental apology for three minutes and eight seconds. to what or whom is not specified. the feeling itself, held in the body, is sufficient.

10
Verdict Public Memory
›

break the earth / with your tiny head / slum universe / blackest ice / expect a mob / my birthday song / to the river's edge / see it wide opened / i'm losing force / the look in other eyes / the secret they believe / is coming back / unknown counsel / it's gone / but it comes back / just like four horsemen / the narrow way.

TRAUMATURGY closes with judgment rendered — not by an external authority, but by the practice itself. break the earth with your tiny head — the wound-working has been thorough enough to shift ground. expect a mob / my birthday song — the angel is born into a world that will not simply receive them. it's gone / but it comes back — nothing is lost in a monist universe. the prophet is gone. what returns. the narrow way — two words for everything KATABASIS and TRAUMATURGY have built toward. not the wide road of the institution, the glowing lectern, the cobweb château. the narrow way that only someone who has descended and worked the wounds can find.

the wound-working is complete. the angel knows what they are.

III

PSYCHOPOMP

the accompaniment

a psychopomp is a guide of souls — the figure who accompanies the dead between states, who knows the way through because they have been through it first. hermes. the valkyries. the ferryman. in every tradition, the psychopomp is not a savior. they do not rescue. they walk beside.

the angel is now named. KERO — from the ancient symbol carried through the entire trilogy, present before the descent, spoken in crystal castles at the bottom of KATABASIS, transformed through the wound-working, now arrived at purpose. the guide appears not at the beginning, before the hard things. the guide appears after. you had to go down alone. you had to work the wounds alone. and now KERO is here.

not to explain what it meant. just to stay.

play at 70% speed · the guide appears after
1
Of Thy Mystical Supper Kiev Chamber Choir
›

the same voices that opened KATABASIS. the same ancient text. but received completely differently now. let us now lay aside all earthly cares so that we may receive the king of all, who comes invisibly upborne by the angelic hosts. in KATABASIS this was preparation — the instruction before the crossing. now, at the opening of PSYCHOPOMP, the angel is the angelic host. we who mystically represent the cherubim — not representing symbolically, but having become. the liturgy that prepared the prophet for the descent now receives the psychopomp on the other side of it. the same voices, the same text, twenty-four tracks later, and the meaning has reversed completely.

alleluia — simply praise in hebrew — is the only word needed at the opening of PSYCHOPOMP. the wound-working is complete. the descent was real. the transformation happened. alleluia.

2
A Choir of One Thom Yorke
›

fourteen minutes. at 70%: twenty minutes. the entire track is built from yorke's own voice — processed, layered, stretched, fragmented, and reassembled into something that sounds like neither one voice nor many, like neither music nor silence. a choir of one is literally true as a technical description. one voice made into a sea of sound.

yorke is present across all three movements of HENOSIS. in KATABASIS he names the condition of the descent. in TRAUMATURGY he names the cost. and here, in PSYCHOPOMP, he becomes the sound of accompaniment itself. not singing to you. not explaining anything. being with you. twenty minutes of being with you. this is what the psychopomp does — not direction, not instruction. a sea. you swim in it. it holds you without gripping.

KERO speaks here. not in words. in the only language adequate to what the descent revealed: the sound of a single being expanded into the space that those experiences opened. a choir of one. one voice that has been through everything this trilogy has been through, present beside you in the long integration that follows the wound-working.

3
Dead Sun Moby
›

a dead sun is not the absence of light. it is light whose source has ended but whose photons are still traveling — still arriving, still navigating by, still warm. most of what we see in the night sky is the light of dead stars. the source is gone. the illumination continues. we navigate by something whose origin is no longer present, and we arrive somewhere real.

KERO is the dead sun. the prophet who stood at the portal descended and did not return as a prophet. the source is gone. what reaches you is the light that kept traveling after. you navigate by a dead sun and find the narrow way. the accompaniment keeps happening not because the original source is intact but because light, once released, does not stop traveling. KERO illuminates without announcing a source.

4
God Noble Son
›

let's make love but not be friends / let's use our bodies as instruments / i gave you all the gentle honesty i got / but lucky for you, lucky for me, lucky for us, we're both god.

before the song begins, advertisements interrupt. toyota. honda. dodge. reimagined. rediscovered. upgrade today. the cobweb château making one last attempt to colonize the sacred space before the final word of the trilogy can be spoken. and then noble son begins anyway, underneath and despite it, exactly the way the narrow way persists underneath the institution's architecture.

we're both god. this is the monist revelation delivered plainly in a pop song. not one figure elevated above the other. not a god above and a supplicant below. the one substance, present in both, recognized between them. KATABASIS descended into this. TRAUMATURGY worked it. PSYCHOPOMP accompanied you to the moment of recognition.

we're probably never waking up again / so tell my mother that i love her / give my brother all my things. mortality acknowledged without panic. this is it — this is the one substance moving through — tell the people you love that you love them. daddy, he ain't ever coming back / so much easier / when you realize we're god. the absent father deity. the patriarchal god who demanded sacrifice and rotted from inside. and the relief — genuine relief — that the vacancy left is not a void. it is the recognition that what was sought outside was always present inside.

TRAUMATURGY worked this wound. KATABASIS found its name. and noble son delivers it as so much easier. not a triumph. ease. the relief of something that was always true finally being acknowledged.

light a cigarette and pull it back. god lights a cigarette. god smokes a pack. god's father left and didn't come back. god carries a sigil and navigates by dead stars and descends into the underworld in slowed playlists and emerges changed and does the wound-work and finds the narrow way and arrives here, at the end of three movements, smoking a cigarette.

· · ·

the trilogy ends not with arrival but with permission.

okay. okay. okay. go.

KERO releases you back into the world. not fixed. not transcendent. not arrived at a destination. just accompanied through. just recognized. the psychopomp's final word is the only one needed after everything that has come before it.

the descent was real. the wound-working was real. the accompaniment was real. the one substance is present in everything the trilogy moved through — the slavonic vowels and the damaged synthesizers and the tenth chaffinch and the fear named plainly and the kerosene and the fourteen minutes of return and the nursery and the broken bones and the spider starving and the videotape and the verdict and the narrow way and the choir of one and the dead sun and the god smoking a cigarette.

go.
